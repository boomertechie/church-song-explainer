# Because He Lives (Amen)

## What This Song Teaches Us About God

The foundation of this song is the resurrection — not as a religious idea but as the ground of everything Christians believe and how they face every stage of life. Because Jesus rose, death doesn't have the final word. The phrase "I know He holds the future" is a statement about God's sovereignty: we aren't at the mercy of chance, we're in the hands of a God who raised His Son from the dead and promises to do the same for all who belong to Him.

In biblical usage "Amen" it means something like "Yes, this is true and I stake my life on it." When the congregation sings it, they're declaring that the resurrection is real, that it matters, and that it changes how they face their own lives and deaths.

## Scripture Connections

- **1 Corinthians 15:20** — Paul calls the risen Jesus "the firstfruits" of those who have died, meaning His resurrection is the guarantee that all who belong to Him will also rise.
- **John 11:25–26** — Jesus tells Martha directly, "I am the resurrection and the life. Whoever believes in me, though he die, yet shall he live."
- **Romans 8:11** — The same Spirit who raised Jesus from the dead lives in every believer, making the promise of resurrection personal and present.
