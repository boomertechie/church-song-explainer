# Lift High the Name of Jesus

## What This Song Teaches Us About God

This song declares that Jesus is not a private religious figure — He is Lord over everything, and His name deserves to be proclaimed publicly and boldly. His name carries weight because of what He has actually done: He conquered sin and death, was exalted to the highest place. When we "lift high" His name, we are not adding to His greatness — we are acknowledging what is already true.

The song also carries a strong call to unity. The vision it holds out is of a gathered community from every nation who have found their identity and hope in the same person. We are not united by preferences or personalities, but by the name of Jesus.

## Scripture Connections

- **Philippians 2:9-11** — God has exalted Jesus to the highest place and given Him the name above every name, so that every knee will bow and every tongue confess that He is Lord.
- **Acts 4:12** — There is no other name under heaven by which we must be saved — the name of Jesus is not one option among many, but the only way to God.
- **Psalm 34:3** — "Glorify the Lord with me; let us exalt his name together" — communal, public praise for God's name is woven throughout Scripture.
