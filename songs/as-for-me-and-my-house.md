# As for Me and My House

## What This Song Teaches Us About God

This song draws from Joshua's public declaration before the nation of Israel — a whole-household, whole-hearted commitment to follow God. It puts that declaration in our mouths. Following God is a decision made actively, repeatedly, and out loud. The song pushes back against the idea that faith is just a personal preference — it frames the household as a place where God's lordship is intentionally established.

The boldness here is also a celebration. When a family commits to honoring God at the center of their home, that's not a burden — it's a foundation. There's peace and purpose in that kind of rooted commitment.

## Scripture Connections
- **Joshua 24:15** — The direct source of the song's central declaration, where Joshua calls Israel to choose whom they will serve and commits his own household to the Lord
- **Deuteronomy 6:6-7** — God's instruction to teach His commands to our children at home, turning the household into a place of constant, natural discipleship
- **Psalm 101:2** — David's commitment to live with integrity inside his own house, reflecting the same spirit of intentional household devotion
