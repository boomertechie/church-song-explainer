# Jesus Strong and Kind

## What This Song Teaches Us About God

This song is built from direct promises Jesus made about Himself — the bread of life, the living water, the light of the world — framed as the answer to the question every person asks in a moment of need: *will anyone help me?*

The pairing of strength and kindness matters. Strength without kindness produces a God you fear but cannot approach. Kindness without strength produces a God who sympathizes but cannot save. Jesus is both, fully. He is powerful enough to save us from sin and death, and gentle enough to welcome the weak and the failing. The song doesn't demand emotional strength from the singer, rather it points to Christ's own words: He will not turn you away, He will not break a bruised reed, He invites the weary to come and find rest.

## Scripture Connections
- **John 6:35** — "I am the bread of life; whoever comes to me shall not hunger, and whoever believes in me shall never thirst" — Jesus' own claim that the song builds on
- **Matthew 11:28-29** — Jesus' direct invitation: "Come to me, all who are weary and burdened, and I will give you rest" — the spirit the song captures throughout
- **Isaiah 42:3** — "A bruised reed he will not break" — the gentleness of Christ toward the weak and struggling that the song's kindness points to
